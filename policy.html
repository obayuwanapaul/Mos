Local Content Policy

MOS ENERGY COMPANY LIMITED(MOSEnergy) has a progressive hiring and sub contracting policy that gives preference to Nigerian citizens including staff, associates,and contractors. Since inception,we have provided internships,attachment and research opportunities for undergraduate, post graduate and faculty of Nigerian universities and other institutions of higher learning. In addition, we frequently participate in training programme such as seminars, lectures and symposia

Inline with the Federal Government of Nigeria’s Local Content Development Policy, MOSENERGY hereby pledges the following

To maximize the participation of indigenous Nigerians in all phases of the Company’s projects.

To use to the extent possible Nigerian expertise for the execution of oil industry projects.

To do an exhaustive search for local expertise before resorting to expatriates for projects in Nigeria. If the need to use foreign contractors should arise,the whole process shall be documented including advertisements placed,enquires made,and reasons why the Company had to resort to foreign contractors.

In cases where the required expertise necessary for project execution is unavailablelocally, thefollowingshall apply:

  • To ensure that our Nigerian employees, associates and/or subcontractors are attached to foreign experts so as to receive the necessary training.
  • To ensure to the extent possible that such expatriates are located in Nigeria for the duration of the project.
  • To put plans in place to ensure that applicable technology is transferred to Nigeria as soon as possible
  • To make available such knowledge and/or technology to the Nigerian oil industry through seminars, workshops and other training programs.

To put in a place a capacity development programme for our Nigerian staff that

  • Assesses their training and educational needs on a continuous basis
  • Provides on-the-job and external continuing education

    • To contribute to the capacity of Nigerians in the oil and gas industry by providing trainingand other educational programmes using various mediasuch as:

  • Class room training
  • Train thetrainer approach
  • Internet (Online)Courses/ DistanceLearning
  • Video, DVDs, CDs etc.

    • To ensure that our hiring policies are free of discrimination and open to all Nigerians regardless of tribe,creed,socio-political orientation,religion,gender etc.

    To make a special effort to attract,hire,retain and promote women and other disadvantaged and/or minority groups. To ensure that women receive the same level ofopportunities foradvancement within the company.

    To create an working environment that is conducive to personal and professional development:

    To invest a reasonable percentage of revenuesintechnologyandother infrastructurethat increases theknowledgebaseofNigerian workers

    To encourage life-long learning in all staff by investing in their training and development

    To provide books,literature,journals,and other learning materials that increase capacity of all Nigerian staff.

    To sponsor,to the extent possible,staff members to oil and gas symposia, exhibitions, lectures, seminars etc.

    To continuously review our Local Content Policy to ensure that it is not only in line with Government policy but also meeting intended objectives and to make changes asappropriate.

    Training Policy

    MOS ENERGY COMPANY LIMITED is committed to fully developing the intellectual capacity of its employees. To this effect, the Company invests a percentage of annual revenues in the training of all staff. The overall objective includes but is not limited to the following:

    • To empower staff to continually develop themselves professionally
    • To ensure the delivery of high quality services to clients
    • To keep employees’ skills up to date and expose them to new technologies
    • To contribute to the development of Nigeria through technology transfer
    • To increase Nigeria Content in the oil and gas Industry
    • To keep up with the rapid pace of technological change of today’s digital age
